Urban gem on Commencement Bay

Annie Wright occupies 10 acres in Tacoma's historic North End neighborhood, and its location is one of the things families either love immediately or find limiting. The campus sits on a bluff overlooking Commencement Bay and Puget Sound, giving students some of the most scenic views of any school in the Pacific Northwest. The historic main building — a stately red-brick structure completed in 1924 — anchors the campus and connects directly to dorms, the dining hall, classrooms, and athletic facilities. Students rarely need to go outside to move between buildings, which matters in rainy Tacoma winters.

The urban setting means Tacoma's cafes, museums, and arts scene are genuinely accessible — and Seattle is 35 miles north. For students who want a city as part of their education, this is a real asset. The tradeoff is density: there's no rolling countryside, no vast playing fields, no sense of remove from the outside world. The school partially mitigates this by partnering with adjacent Garfield Park and the Tacoma Lawn Tennis Club for outdoor space.

A major renovation wave since 2017 has significantly modernized the campus. A new all-weather James Memorial turf field was completed in 2017, a 26,000 sq ft academic building for the boys' upper school opened in 2019, and a new third gym and 25-yard underground pool were added the same year. Most recently (2023–2024), the former basement pool was transformed into creative arts studios — a recording studio, textile lab, dance studio, and music rehearsal room. A fitness center renovation is completing in 2025.

Recent investment highlight $500,000 raised at the 2023 Crab Feed fundraiser for arts hub renovation; a seven-figure gift from the H.E. Anderson Foundation funded the new student lounge and middle school arts facilities.

One of America's great boarding school campuses

Mercersburg's 300-acre rural campus in Franklin County, Pennsylvania is, without exaggeration, one of the most beautiful secondary school settings in the country. The campus has the feel of a small New England liberal arts college — stone buildings, a Gothic chapel that dominates the skyline, sweeping playing fields, tree-lined paths, and the quiet of the Appalachian highlands surrounding it. About 90 miles northwest of Washington D.C., the school is remote enough to create genuine immersion while remaining accessible by car.

The architectural centerpiece is the Chapel, designed by the firm Cram & Ferguson — the same architects responsible for Princeton's chapel — and it sets a tone of serious institutional purpose that permeates the whole campus. Seven student dormitories house the 81% boarding population, and the school's infrastructure is comprehensive: its own library, arts center, college counseling center, student center, dining hall, alumni center, and multiple dedicated athletic complexes. Students rarely need to leave the grounds and, during the week, largely don't.

The $453M endowment — one of the ten highest per-student at any independent secondary school in the U.S. — keeps everything in exceptional condition. Unlike many schools that struggle to fund facility maintenance, Mercersburg continually invests in its physical plant through donor gifts that are often fully covered by a single family, as with the recent $600K squash court renovation.

Notable campus landmark The James Buchanan Cabin — believed to be the birthplace of the only U.S. president from Pennsylvania — was purchased by the school in 1953 and stands near Nolde Gymnasium on campus.

Academic spaces

The 2019 Boys Academic Building is the most significant facility addition in Annie Wright's recent history. Designed by Seattle firm Mithun, the 26,000 sq ft three-story structure houses advanced science laboratories, flexible IB-oriented classrooms, a design and makerspace, and connects to the new gym and pool. It's a genuinely contemporary building — high-performance envelope, daylight harvesting, natural ventilation, outdoor learning spaces — and it reflects the school's commitment to a modern learning environment rather than resting on historical charm alone.

Athletics facilities

Annie Wright has three gyms: the original Kemper Gym (freshly renovated with new court surfaces), a second gym, and the new 2019 facility with its own basketball court. The 25-yard Barbey-Murray underground pool opened in 2019. A major 2025 fitness center expansion adds a triple-sized athletic training room, updated locker rooms, a cutting-edge weight room, a multipurpose studio, and separate student and faculty fitness rooms. For a school of 370 upper school students on 10 acres, the facility provision is genuinely impressive.

Arts spaces

The Kemper Theatre is a 350-seat proscenium-style venue — professional-quality for student productions. Music practice rooms support individual and group lessons. In 2023–2024, the former basement pool space became a recording studio, textile lab, dance studio, and music and theatre rehearsal room — a creative arts hub that reflects real investment in the performing arts, not just a multipurpose room.

Boarding facilities

The dorms are accessed directly from the main building, meaning boarding students can move between living quarters, classrooms, dining, and the gym without stepping outside. Rooms are described as spacious and comfortable with natural light. Students share kitchen facilities, media lounges, and study rooms within the dorm wing.

The Goldthorpe Athletic Complex

Mercersburg's athletic facilities are in a different league from most prep schools. The Goldthorpe Athletic Complex is 209,000 square feet of indoor athletic and training space — to put that in perspective, that's more square footage than many Division III college athletic centers. It contains: Nolde Gymnasium (three full basketball courts, wrestling center, climbing wall, aerobics studio, fitness center), the Lloyd Aquatic Center (Olympic 50-meter, 10-lane pool with diving well; seating for 500 spectators), the Hale Field House (62,000 sq ft — the largest indoor fieldhouse in the Mid-Atlantic Prep League, housing a 200-meter competition track with 6 turn lanes and 8 sprint lanes), and the Davenport Squash Center (10 state-of-the-art ASB courts with live-streaming and performance analytics, fully refurbished in 2025 for ~$600K).

Outdoor athletic facilities

Ten grass playing fields cover baseball, field hockey, lacrosse (boys and girls), soccer (boys and girls), and softball. Regents' Field provides a synthetic turf surface for field hockey and lacrosse. Cannon Field is the varsity baseball facility with covered dugouts, warm-up areas, and a scoreboard. The Smoyer Tennis Center handles tennis. The sheer scale and quality of the outdoor athletic infrastructure is remarkable for a school of 450 students.

The Burgin Center for the Arts

The Burgin Center opened in 2006 (with Benicio del Toro attending the grand opening) and provides dedicated, purpose-built space for the school's performing and fine arts programs. The Simon Theatre is the primary performance venue. The arts center houses the Stony Batter Players drama program — founded in 1899 — as well as the school's nine music, dance, and theatre ensembles.

Academics

Depends on the student

The IB framework — a distinctive academic identity

Annie Wright's commitment to the International Baccalaureate curriculum is the defining academic fact about the school. It's the only institution in Washington state offering the full IB continuum from preschool through Grade 12, which means students arrive at the upper school already versed in the IB's philosophy — inquiry-based, internationally minded, assessment-driven rather than test-driven. The Diploma Programme in Grades 11–12 is respected worldwide, not just in U.S. college admissions, and it particularly opens doors at universities in Canada, the UK, and Europe.

The IB DP structure is deliberately balanced: students must take subjects from six groups (language, second language, individuals and societies, sciences, mathematics, arts), write a 4,000-word Extended Essay on a topic of their choice, complete a Theory of Knowledge course, and fulfill a Creativity, Activity, Service (CAS) requirement. This is a rigorous, holistic framework that rewards students who think across disciplines and write well.

The tradeoff is less menu flexibility. Students on the full diploma track follow a prescribed structure with limited room to pursue hyper-specialized electives. The school's 8:1 ratio and average class size of 11 mean individual attention is strong. Average ACT score of 34 ranks Annie Wright among the top 20 boarding schools nationally on standardized test performance.

Breadth, depth, and an extraordinary faculty ratio

Mercersburg's academic model is the traditional elite college-prep approach, executed at a very high level. With 150+ courses and 40+ honors/advanced studies offerings, students have genuine latitude to pursue interests deeply. The 4:1 student-faculty ratio is the headline number — among the very best at any boarding school in the country, rivaling schools with far larger endowments. In practice, this means small seminars, real faculty mentorship, and the kind of one-on-one academic relationships that transform students.

The school's curriculum includes two senior capstone programs that give students real research and project experience before college, which bridges the gap between high school academic work and genuine intellectual independence. A robotics program, global travel opportunities, and 30+ student clubs round out the intellectual life. Faculty have dedicated their careers to working with teenagers; the depth of their investment is regularly cited by alumni as the defining feature of the Mercersburg experience.

College placement is exceptional: approximately 68% of recent graduates gained admission to a top-50 national university or liberal arts college, and 20–25% of each graduating class is recruited for college athletics. The school competes in the Mid-Atlantic Prep League alongside Blair, Hill, Lawrenceville, and Peddie — academically rigorous peer institutions.

Boarding Life & Community

Mercersburg wins

Flexible boarding with real community roots

Annie Wright's approach to boarding is deliberately flexible and community-integrated, which sets it apart from traditional boarding schools. Students choose between 5-day boarding (going home Friday after school, returning Sunday evening) or 7-day residential. About half the upper school boards; the other half commutes as day students. This means the school has genuine roots in the Tacoma community and isn't a closed-off world.

The boarding program is structured around dorm groups of 16–17 students, each with one or two prefects and a live-in dorm parent. Eight residential faculty oversee the full program, and the stated mission explicitly balances academics, wellness, and character. Boarders live within the main building — the same building as their classrooms and dining hall — creating a genuinely tight-knit residential experience despite the urban setting. Weekend activities range from outdoor adventure to movie nights and community events.

International students represent 26% of the school, and the 5-day option is explicitly available to domestic students while international students requiring F-1 visas enroll in the 7-day program. For families looking for an accessible, lower-commitment entry into boarding school, Annie Wright's structure is genuinely appealing.

Total immersion — this is what boarding school means

At Mercersburg, boarding isn't an option — it's the identity of the school. With 81% of students living on campus, the community is fully residential in character. The rural location ensures genuine immersion; there's no slipping off to the suburbs for the weekend. The result is the kind of total community formation that boarding school advocates describe: shared traditions, deep faculty relationships, lifelong friendships forged in close proximity, and a student who graduates having navigated five years of genuine independence within a structured environment.

Seven dormitories house students by grade and gender, with faculty and their families living on campus in faculty housing. The "family feel" described by alumni isn't marketing language — it reflects a reality where students see the same faculty member in class, at dinner, and coaching the JV lacrosse team. When Benicio del Toro returned to campus in April 2025, he addressed students over dinner and screened a film in the theatre. That kind of sustained institutional relationship with alumni is only possible in a boarding community this tightly woven.

Traditions run deep: Step Songs (an annual pep rally tradition where students gather on the steps of Main Hall to sing school songs for visiting alumni) dates back to the early 1900s. Alumni Weekend draws graduates back every fall, and the alumni network is described by families as impressively active and genuinely useful after graduation.

Athletics & Outdoor Programs

Mercersburg wins

GOAT program: the Pacific Northwest as classroom

Annie Wright competes as a 1A school in the Nisqually League — a smaller competitive division appropriate for a school of 370 upper school students on a 10-acre campus. The school offers 10 interscholastic sports including basketball, soccer, football (a relatively unusual offering for a small private school), volleyball, tennis, golf, and track. The school's athletic program has expanded meaningfully with the 2019 gym and pool additions, and the 2025 fitness center upgrade further raises the standard.

The genuine differentiator at Annie Wright isn't varsity competition — it's the GOAT (Great Outdoor Adventure Team) program. The Pacific Northwest is one of the most geographically spectacular places on earth, and the GOAT program uses it deliberately: students summit Mt. St. Helens, white-water raft Pacific Northwest rivers, rock climb, kayak on Puget Sound, and explore Mount Rainier and Olympic National Parks. Students earn certificates along the way, and the program runs through the academic year, not just as occasional field trips. For a student who came to Annie Wright partly for the PNW setting, GOAT is a genuine selling point.

29 sports, 24 Olympians, best-in-class facilities

Mercersburg's athletic program is one of the strongest at any boarding school in the country. The 29 interscholastic sports span the full range from traditional (baseball, football, lacrosse, soccer, basketball, swimming, tennis, golf) to competitive niche sports (squash, wrestling, field hockey, diving, cross country) to adventure sports (climbing, kayaking, mountain biking, skiing, snowboarding, cycling). The school competes in the Mid-Atlantic Prep League — one of the most competitive prep school leagues in the country — against institutions like Blair Academy, The Hill School, Lawrenceville, Peddie, and Hun School.

The swimming program has produced more than two dozen Olympians, anchored by the Lloyd Aquatic Center's Olympic 50-meter pool. The squash program is nationally prominent: the Davenport Center's 10 courts (just refurbished in 2025 for ~$600K) are considered one of the best high school squash facilities on the East Coast, and the boys' program has won the MAPL squash championship multiple years running. Typically 20–25% of each graduating class is recruited for college athletics — an exceptional placement rate.

The outdoor education program — skiing, mountain biking, climbing, kayaking — mirrors Annie Wright's GOAT in spirit, but within a different geographic context (the Appalachians and mid-Atlantic rather than the Cascades).

Arts & Performance

Strong at both — edge to Mercersburg

Arts at Annie Wright benefit from being embedded in the IB curriculum, not bolted on as extracurriculars. Visual arts, music, and theatre carry academic weight and are part of the Diploma Programme's assessment structure. This means arts students are evaluated with the same rigor as science students, and the skills developed (critical analysis, extended commentary, exhibition) transfer well to college arts programs.

The school runs three full productions per year (all-school, upper school, and middle/lower school), and extracurricular offerings are surprisingly broad for a school of this size: music production, theatre tech, improv comedy, textiles, orchestra (including zero-period orchestra for early risers), and a 350-seat Kemper Theatre to perform in. The 2023–24 arts hub expansion added a recording studio, textile lab, and dedicated dance and rehearsal spaces — a significant investment in creative infrastructure.

Arts highlights
✓Arts embedded in IB curriculum — academic assessment, not just extracurricular
✓3 full productions/year; 350-seat Kemper Theatre proscenium stage

Mercersburg's performing arts tradition begins in 1899 with the founding of Stony Batter Players — the oldest continually active student organization on campus. The lineage is extraordinary: Jimmy Stewart '28 performed in Stony Batter before going on to win the Academy Award for Best Actor, and Benicio del Toro '85 won the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor for Traffic. Grammy-nominated record producer Luke Ebbin is also an alum. This isn't historical trivia; it reflects a school that has genuinely produced world-class artistic talent, and that tradition shapes the culture today.

The Burgin Center for the Arts opened in 2006 providing a purpose-built home for the school's nine music, dance, and theatre ensembles. The Simon Theatre is the primary performance venue. Visual arts, music, and theatre are all staffed by dedicated faculty, and the scale of the arts program — nine ensembles — is remarkable for a school of 450 students. Fine arts, ceramics, photography, and digital media round out the visual arts offerings.

Tuition, Aid & Endowment

Mercersburg wins

Annie Wright's 2025–26 boarding tuition of $81,085 is actually slightly higher than Mercersburg's, which is notable given the difference in institutional scale and endowment. The school offers need-based financial aid through the Clarity Application platform, and rolling admissions means families who miss the January 24 deadline can still apply and receive aid consideration. The school's $36 million endowment, while not insignificant for a school of its size, limits the depth of aid available compared to larger-endowed institutions.

For families who need substantial financial assistance, Annie Wright's aid program may be more constrained than peer schools. The school does not publicly disclose the percentage of students receiving aid or the total aid budget. Day tuition at $37,520 offers a considerably lower entry point for local Tacoma families, and the flexible 5-day boarding option creates a middle ground for families who want some of the boarding experience at lower cost.

$81,085 boarding 2025–26 $37,520 day $36M endowment Rolling admissions

Mercersburg's financial aid story is genuinely exceptional. The $453 million endowment funds a program that reaches approximately half of all enrolled families — double the typical boarding school average of ~30%. Last year the school awarded nearly $12 million in aid, ranging from a few thousand dollars to full tuition remission based on demonstrated need. The school's stated goal is to never let financial barriers prevent a gifted student from attending.

Named scholarships include the Legacy Scholar (full boarding tuition for children/grandchildren of alumni), the Preparatory Scholar (full tuition for outstanding day students), and the 1893 Scholar (awarded from the general applicant pool for exceptional citizenship). Access Scholarships specifically target gifted students from underrepresented backgrounds. For families who might assume a school at this price point is out of reach, the reality is that Mercersburg may be more affordable than it appears — and for exceptional students, potentially free.
